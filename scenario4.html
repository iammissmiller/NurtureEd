Social Media Pressure Case

Riya, a class 9 student, spends a lot of time on social media after school. Over time, she starts following popular creators and classmates who post short videos. Recently, she noticed a growing trend where students perform risky challenges to gain likes, comments, and followers. Some of her classmates gained sudden popularity, and their posts were widely shared.

Riya begins to feel left out as her own posts receive little attention. She starts believing that she also needs to follow these trends to be noticed. Even though she feels uncomfortable and knows the activity might be unsafe, the desire to fit in and gain approval pushes her toward making a risky decision. She thinks that if she doesn’t participate, she might lose her social relevance among peers.

Review & Insights

B. Peer pressure online (Correct)

Correct because the student is being influenced by others’ actions and expectations. The desire to fit in and gain approval is driving the decision, which is a clear sign of peer pressure in an online environment.

A. Creativity and self-expression

This is incorrect because the student is not acting out of originality or personal expression. Instead, the behavior is influenced by external pressure and a need for validation, which removes genuine creativity from the decision-making process.

C. Entertainment and fun

Although these trends may appear entertaining, the student’s motivation is not enjoyment. The decision is driven by fear of missing out and the need for social acceptance, making it a pressure-based action rather than fun.

D. Building confidence

True confidence comes from making independent and safe choices. In this case, the student is relying on external validation through likes and attention, which can actually reduce self-confidence over time.
