Reddit Friend Case

Arjun, a student, joins an online discussion on Reddit where students talk about academic stress and personal challenges. He comes across a user who seems very understanding and supportive. Over several days, they chat regularly, and the person listens to his problems, offers advice, and builds a sense of trust.

As the conversations continue, the person begins to say things like, “I trust you a lot. You can trust me too.” Gradually, they start asking for personal details such as Arjun’s school name, location, and even photos. Arjun feels confused because the person has been kind and helpful, and he doesn’t want to seem rude. At the same time, he feels uncomfortable sharing such information but is unsure how to respond.

Review & Insights

C. Stop sharing and avoid giving personal information (Correct)

Correct because protecting personal information is essential. When someone starts asking for sensitive details, it is safest to stop sharing and limit interaction to prevent potential misuse or harm.

A. Share details because they seem trustworthy

This is unsafe because online identities can be fake. Even if someone appears kind and supportive, they may have hidden intentions. Sharing personal information can lead to misuse, including privacy breaches or manipulation.

B. Continue chatting but avoid personal topics

This may seem safer, but continuing interaction still carries risk. The person has already crossed boundaries, and further communication could increase pressure or lead to more attempts to gain personal information.

D. Share limited information to test trust

Even small pieces of information can be combined to identify someone. Testing trust by sharing details is risky, as it still exposes personal data that could be used in harmful ways.
